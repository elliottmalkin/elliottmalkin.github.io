Case Study: Discovery

Readers flock to The New York Times for its curated selection of news from editors rather than algorithms. But The Times produces a firehose of great content every day, and only a small portion of it is Top News. How do you surface the remainder?

I proposed a sort of Tinder for news that would rely on a strong recommendation engine then under development.

See full concept »

When the recommendation quality couldn't support it, I suggested we pivot to a simpler solution. It made use of two basic pieces of information: an aggregate notion of the most popular stories, and which of those stories a user has seen.

The outcome was In Case You Missed It, a deduped list of trending stories.

For Treelines, a product concept for the genealogy market, I developed a style of visualization that married family trees and timelines.

Children (green) are nested under parents as both descendents and events in time.

As children branch out from their families of origin and start households of their own, they transition from green to pink or blue.
